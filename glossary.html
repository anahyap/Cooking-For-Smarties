Glossary/Index

  • Sauté: Cook quickly in a small amount of hot fat over medium-high heat, stirring or tossing often.
  • Marinate: Soak food in a seasoned liquid to add flavor and tenderize before cooking.
  • Sear: Brown the surface of food in a very hot pan to develop flavor and crust.
  • Simmer: Cook in liquid just below a boil, with small, gentle bubbles (about 185–205°F / 85–96°C).
    • For more examples of simmering techniques, visit Serious Eats where they break down heat levels in detail.
  • Boil: Cook in rapidly bubbling liquid (212°F / 100°C at sea level).
  • Braise: Brown food, then cook it covered with a small amount of liquid over low heat until tender.
  • Roast: Cook uncovered with dry heat (usually in an oven) to brown and caramelize.
  • Broil: Cook with intense direct heat from above to brown and char quickly.
  • Deglaze: Add liquid to a hot pan to dissolve browned bits (fond) for a quick sauce.
  • Dice: Cut into small, even cubes (typically ¼–½ inch / 6–12 mm).
  • Mince: Chop very finely into tiny, uniform pieces.
  • Zest: Finely grate the colored outer peel of citrus to add aroma and flavor.
  • Fold: Gently combine mixtures using a spatula to keep air from deflating.
  • Reduce: Simmer a liquid to evaporate water and concentrate flavor and thickness.
  • Blanch: Briefly boil food, then shock in ice water to stop cooking and set color/texture.
